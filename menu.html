Preparing the Ewedu Soup

Pick your Ewedu Leaves, make sure you don't pick the stalk/stem along with it.


Step-1: Wash the leaves thoroughly to remove dirt. Pour the water in a pot, boil it up and add in your Ewedu Leaves.

Step-2: If you are using a broom(Ijabe), use the broom to pound the leaves in the pot.

Step-3: If you are using a blender, take the Ewedu off the heat and leave to cook for a bit... don't switch off the heat yet.

Step-4: Add the Ewedu into a blender along with the Iru, make sure you don't add all the water. Just 1/2(half) a cup of water should do. Too much water will make your Ewedu watery.

Step-5: Increase the heat a few times till you get a semi-thick Ewedu mixture.

Step-6: Now its time to add in your bouillion cube, and salt. Try to get the right amount of salt needed.

Step-7: Switch off the heat and let it simmer with the remaining heat for for 3 minutes.... Keep stirring to prevent burning. Do not over cook or the Ewedu will go brown.

Your Ewedu is now fully prepared. Check below on how to prepare the Amala

Preparing the Amala

Step-1: Pour water into the pot and allow to get hot.

Step-2: Add the yam flour into it and stir continuously using a wookden stick(omorogun in Yoruba) to avoid lumps.

Step-3: Add extra Yam flour if watery or add some water if its too strong(just like the way you make your semovita).

Your Food is Ready! Enjoy
