Preparing Pap(Ogi or Akamu)

funny-tip: This one meal, keeps disgracing a lot of People when they get it wrong, Do not add too much water, be careful so you don't ruin it.


Step-1: Wash your hands and put all of the ingredients in one place.

Step-2: Put some lumps of the akamu/ogi/pap into a bowl. Akamu rises during during preparation so you should a bowl big enough to contain the meal.

Step-3: Use a table spoon to break down the lumps of ogi into small pieces.

Step-4: Add cool water in quantities and mix till you have no lumps in it. You should be aiming to mix the akamu as thick as possible.

Step-5: In the same containing the oil, add in oil and when the oil shimmers, add in onions, curry powder, your thym, bay leaves and a large pinch of salt and pepper. Keep stirring over a mediu heat until the onion becomes translucent(for about 2 minutes).

Step-6: Start boiling your water, and ensure its more than enough; when preparing pap, it is better to prepare too much water than to no not have enough waterwhen making the pap.

Step-7: Before the water boils, stir the mixture very well because some of the pap may have settled at the bottom of the bowl. If not stirred well, it will cause lumps when you start making it.

Step-8: Once the water boild, pour it slowly and steadilyin a circular(round) motion into the bowl of akamu and stir at the same time. This is very important to remove lumps.

Step-9: Once you see the mixture setting, stop stirring and reduce the amount of water you are pouring till the pap has completely set.

Step-10: Set the kettle aside and stir the pap very well. If its too thick for you, you can add in mor ehot water but you should be very careful so it does not become watery. Making it watery isn't good; remember that you will still add in evaporated milk.

Step-11: Add in Evaporated milk if you have one and if you don't its fine. Stir everything to the way you like it.

Step-12: Now is the right time to add in your sugar or honey and startadjust it to your liking.

Voila, Thats the end. enjoy your meal :)
